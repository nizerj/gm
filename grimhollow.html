The City of Liesech

"No knowledge should be feared, because knowledge is power."
Once a wealthy trade hub in Bite Bay, Liesech is now a quarantined nightmare. The Weeping Pox rots the living, while the Morbus Doctore experiment on the dying.

The Rotting History Deals in the Dark

The Bouwengracht Bargain
Generations ago, the fisherman Otto Bouwengracht made a pact with the Filth Gazer, a creature in the bay. The creature consumed the city's filth and corpses; in exchange, the family received wealth and power.
The Red Morning
The Vampire Countess Analita von Raiza infiltrated the city to seize it for the Bürach Empire. Discovering the source of the family's power, she slaughtered the entire Bouwengracht family in a single night to break their bond with the creature.
The Weeping Pox
Enraged by the broken pact, the Filth Gazer infected the city's corpses with a virulent plague.
Symptoms: Sharpened senses, delirium, burning fever, and weltering lesions.

Factions of the Quarantine

Cult of the Black Lady
Fanatics who worship the Vampire Countess Analita. They believe beauty can be found even in the rot. They kidnap non-infected citizens to feed their regenerating mistress in the High Belfry.
Morbus Doctore
Physicians wearing beak masks and strange coats. They believe the Pox is an evolutionary blessing. They wander the streets conducting unbearable experiments on "patients" to bring humanity to a new level of existence.
Golden Barge Company
The last remnant of honorable trade. They transport goods and people along the canals. Neutral and devoted to duty, they are the only safe way to travel—if you have the coin.

Notable Locations

The High Belfry
A massive bell tower that serves as the seat of power. Countess Analita sleeps at the top, regenerating from her battle with the Filth Gazer.
The Eye of the Sea
The center of the bay where the Filth Gazer dwells. No ship returns from here. It is said to be a churning vortex of pollution and tentacles.
The Froth Church
Once the center of the "Cult of the Sea," built on the westernmost quay. A channel allows the sea water (and the creature) to enter directly into the church for dark offerings.

The Bürach Empire

Once the beacon of civilization, now a shattering nation. The Gods are dead, and the Great Beast stalks the countryside. The Empire is divided into four distinct provinces.

Abendland The Heart of the Empire

Altenheim (Capital)
The "City of Joy." The largest city in the Empire and seat of the Imperial Throne. It is sustained by the trade of the entire continent but rots from within due to decadence and the drugs known as Dream Leaves.
Key Entities
  • Emperor Aratron II: The boy emperor, a figurehead controlled by the church.
  • Pope Roland Murrich: Leader of the Hearthkeepers and the true power behind the throne.
  • Gatekeeper Chantal Sommerkin: Leader of the Celestial Gate, a doomsday cult growing in power.
Castle Martorius
The floating, shifting castle of the Archmage Martorius. It appeared after the Gods' End. Martorius advises the crown, but his true motives remain a mystery.

Nordenland The Magical & The Wild

Erlefurt (West Capital)
Loyal to the Empire. Home to the Magister College, the premier academy for arcane magic.
Ulmirac (East Capital)
Independent and rebellious. Home to the Order of the Golden Bough (Druids). They reject Imperial rule and follow the Old Ways.
Key Entities
  • Arch Duchess Maelfa: Ruler of the West, maintaining a delicate peace with the Empire.
  • Patriarch Alfric: Leader of the Oaken Court in the East, keeping outsiders at bay.

Rauland Iron & Industry

Stehlenwald
The city of steel. Once a dwarven stronghold, now the industrial heart of the Empire. The smog here is thick enough to choke on.
Gräffendam
A massive dam city. The rich live on top in the "Gildreath," while the poor rot in the slums below known as "The Shade," controlled by the Ebon Syndicate.
Key Entities
  • Arch Duke Ulrich Eisenherz: A fair dwarven ruler rumored to possess a god's artifact.
  • Artom Drago: A lycanthrope hunter who leads a pack of werewolves to fight the Beast's spawn.

Unterland The Shield of the Empire

Olstenburg
A city of warriors. Home to the Academy of Swords. Every citizen here serves military duty to hold back the Ostoyan undead.
Key Entities
  • Lord General Vassily Roemer: A brilliant but traditional commander focused on the Ostoyan war.
  • Speaker Coran Schlei: Leader of the Cult of Tormach (Daemon of Wrath), seeking to usurp command.

The Ostoyan Empire

A land where the sun never fully rises. The Dark Mist covers the sky, protecting the undead nobility from the light. It is a nation divided: the vampires ruling the south, and the mages holding the north.

Soma The Land of Eternal Night

Nov Ostoya (Capital)
The City of Gargoyles. The massive capital where vampires and humans coexist in a terrified peace. The city is literally built on top of the sinking ruins of an older city, creating the "City Below."
Crow's Bend
A river port city constantly plagued by zombie outbreaks. The river itself is often clogged with corpses floating down from the interior.
Key Entities
  • Grand Duke Drago Koshevek: The vampire ruler of Soma. Cruel but efficient.
  • The Crimson Court: The vampire nobility. They view humans as cattle to be managed, not citizens.
  • The Order of Dawn: A secret organization of hunters dedicated to destroying the undead scourge.

Raevo The Resistance

Castalore
The fortress of mages. The seat of Raevo's power, protected by magical barriers that keep the vampires and the dark mist at bay.
Malkovia
A fortress city on the border. It is the first line of defense against the Bürach Empire and the vampire legions of Soma.
Key Entities
  • General Arakon: The leader of the Raevo military, a tactical genius holding the line.
  • The Ostoyan Mages: Wielders of forbidden magic who believe only fire and arcane might can save humanity.

The Valikan Clans

A harsh, frozen landscape where survival is the only law. The clans are split into two rival nations. Beneath the ice sleeps Gormadraug, the Great Prismatic Wyrm, whose waking would end the world.

Thrull The Raiders

Tyburn
A brutal city of raiders and shipwrights. The economy runs on pillage and slavery. Weakness here is a death sentence.
Key Entities
  • Chief Knan: A ruthless warlord who seeks to unite the clans through strength and conquest.
  • The Rune-Keepers: Mystics who carve magical runes into flesh and stone to harness the power of the cold.

Kandar The Traders

Halsfjord (Capital)
A heavily fortified port city. Unlike Thrull, Kandar seeks trade with the south. They are the shield that protects the civilized world from Thrull's raids.
Solheim
The northernmost reach, home to the Cult of the Wyrm. The ice here is strange, glowing with the sleeping dreams of Gormadraug.
Key Entities
  • Queen Andrea Helsing: The ruler of Kandar, struggling to maintain alliances against the Thrull threat.
  • The Prismatic Circle: Powerful druids who maintain the elemental seals keeping Gormadraug asleep.

The Castinellan Provinces

A land of scorching heat and burning faith. Unified by the warlord Montego Valieda, the three provinces are a militaristic theocracy dedicated to the Arch Seraph Empyreus. Arcane magic is punishable by death.

Toletum The Military Heart

Toletum City
The seat of the Theocracy. A fortress city where children are trained in the spear and bow from age seven. The Ember Forge here never goes cold, churning out weapons for the righteous war.
Key Entities
  • Montego Valieda: "The Uniter." The High Commander who conquered the provinces and forged them into a single nation.
  • The Inquisitors: Zealots who roam the streets looking for signs of arcane corruption.

Faro The Golden Harbor

Faro City
The economic powerhouse. Home to the Great Arsenal shipyard. While Toletum rules the land, Faro rules the sea. The surrounding land, "The Blessing," feeds the entire nation.
Key Entities
  • The Mighty Farosi Fleet: The strongest naval force in Etharis, projecting Castinellan power across the continent.
  • Guild Navigators: The only sailors skilled enough to navigate the treacherous Misty Reefs near Morencia.

Therpena The Dragon's Breath

The Shattered Peaks
Home to the Cathedral of Embers, the headquarters of the Arcanist Inquisition. It is also the ancestral home of the Dragonborn (the Ember Cairn), who serve as the Theocracy's shock troops.
High Cavalry School
Producing the finest mounted warriors in the world. Their heavy cavalry charges are said to shake the earth like an earthquake.
Key Entities
  • Aban Dahlrum: "The Red Horse," a legendary instructor of the cavalry.
  • Arch Seraph Empyreus: The patron deity of the nation. He demands Valor, Truth, and the purging of magic.

The Charneault Kingdom

A realm of chivalry, mystery, and broken oaths. Protected by the Knight Chapters and bound by the Pact of the Sacred Land, humans and elves share this mist-shrouded land. But the spirits are restless, and the Dark Elves stir.

Caer Neiada The Land of Rivers

Castle Lamesdhonneur
The capital, built amidst massive waterfalls. It is the seat of King Thibault de Sauveterre. The "Blade of Honor" is the heart of human power, but it is plagued by political infighting among the Knight Chapters.
Tol Leyemil
The last great Elven city, hidden deep within the forests. It is a place of ancient magic, ruled by the Assembly of Elves. Humans are rarely welcome here, and tensions are high as the Dark Elves infiltrate its society.
Key Entities
  • King Thibault: A weary king trying to save his cursed daughter and hold his fracturing kingdom together.
  • The Bondmakers: Elven shamans who maintain the pact with nature spirits.
  • The Severe Templars: A fanatical knight chapter that hates elves and magic, seeking to purge the land of "fey corruption."

The Mystic Wilds Where the Mists Rule

Temple of Mists
The most sacred site in the kingdom. By ancient pact, the human King must send his firstborn daughter here to become a Bondmaker. Recently, the mists have turned dark, and something ancient is waking up.
Tol Kerdywel
The fallen city of the Dark Elves. A cursed ruin where the Dark Mists originate. It is said that the Dark Elves are massing here to retake their ancestral lands from the humans.
Key Entities
  • The Mist Walkers: Rangers and druids who dare to travel the mists to keep the roads safe.
  • The Unseelie Court: Wicked fey who have been twisted by the dark magic of Tol Kerdywel.

The City-State of Morencia

A frantic web of canals, bridges, and coin. Protected by the Misty Reefs, Morencia has remained independent from the Empires for centuries. It is the bank of Etharis, where the Augustine Trading Company holds more power than kings.

The Powers That Be Coin & Daggers

The Supreme Council
The city is officially ruled by Doge Lorenzo Flabenici and the Council. They maintain the "Illusion of Security"—the belief that Morencia is invincible—to keep the world's gold flowing into their vaults.
Augustine Trading Company
A massive mercantile force that effectively owns the Bankers Guild. They have a controlling interest in the city's politics and mercenaries. If you owe them money, you don't just lose your house; you disappear.
Guild Navigators
The only sailors capable of traversing the magical Misty Reefs without crashing. This monopoly makes them essential to the city's survival and incredibly wealthy.

The Dread of Moonless Night

The Canal Murders
Despite its wealth, Morencia is plagued by a serial killer (or cult) known as the "Dread." On moonless nights, bodies are found floating in the canals, ritually butchered. The Council suppresses this news to maintain the city's reputation.
The Ebon Syndicate
While they operate everywhere, the Syndicate's branch in Morencia is particularly vicious. They control the smuggling rings that bypass the customs of the Guild Navigators.

The Arcanist Inquisition

"Magic is a curse. Fire is the cure."
Founded in the Castinellan Provinces, the Inquisition is a military-religious order dedicated to the Arch Seraph Empyreus. They believe arcane magic caused the Gods' End and must be wiped from Etharis.

The Chain of Command

High Inquisitor
The supreme leader, answering only to the Arch Seraph Empyreus himself. Currently, this position is held by Inquisitor Valieda (the warlord of Castinella), merging church and state into a weapon of war.
The Justicars
The elite knights of the order. Clad in golden plate and wielding flaming weapons, they are the executioners. They are often deployed to hunt high-level targets like Liches or Archmages.
The Finders
Spies and investigators who infiltrate cities to root out secret covens. They often use magical artifacts (sanctioned by the church) to detect the use of illegal magic.

Doctrine & Strongholds

Cathedral of Embers
Located in the Shattered Peaks of Therpena. It is a massive fortress-temple built over an active volcanic vent. The "Eternal Flame" burns here, where captured mages are brought for "purification."
The Rite of Branding
Mages who surrender are not always killed. Some are "Branded"—a painful ritual that severs their connection to the Weave, leaving them alive but powerless and scarred.
The Gray Library
A restricted archive within the Cathedral containing confiscated spellbooks and artifacts. Only High Inquisitors may enter, ostensibly to study the enemy, but rumors suggest they use the power for themselves.

The Thaumaturge

"We who shape magic, shape the world."
A secret confederation of the most powerful Archmages in Etharis. They put aside political borders to prevent world-ending threats (like the Aether Kindred), often acting where governments cannot.

The Hidden Council
Headquarters: A collectively maintained extraplanar space. Members are bound by a powerful geas that prevents them from betraying one another.
Key Members
  • Martorius: The reclusive Archmage of the Floating Castle (Bürach).
  • Mina Taliesin: The Lady of Masks (Ostoya).
  • Odis Tremain: Historian of Sarmar Academy (Charneault).

"Success in all ventures."

Timeline of Etharis

History in Etharis is not a record of triumph, but a eulogy. It is defined by three great tragedies: The Purge of the Elder Races, The Madness of the Emperors, and the Death of the Gods.

I. The Ancient Era

The Primordial Sleep
The legendary hero Kentigern slays the Great Prismatic Wyrm, Gormadraug. Its body forms the northern continent (Grarjord), and its slumber creates the threat of Coldfire.
The Aetheric War
The Gods wage war against the Aether Kindred—eldritch horrors from the void. The Kindred are sealed away, but the magical strain sows the seeds of future madness in the divine artifacts.

II. The Era of Expansion

Arrival of Humanity
Humans migrate to Etharis. Initially dismissed by Elves and Dwarves, they multiply rapidly and master magic and steel.
The Great Purge
Humanity declares total war on the Elder Races.
• Elves: Their kingdoms are burned; survivors flee to the deep forests of Charneault.
• Dwarves: Stehlenwald seals its gates. Grebenstein is destroyed.
• Dragonborn: The Ember Cairn is razed.

III. The Golden Age

Rise of Indorious
Guided by the Four Divines, Emperor Indorious I unites the warring human tribes into the Bürach Empire. The "Golden Age" begins.
The Edict of Eternal Blood
To keep the Gods' blessing, the Hearthkeepers decree that the Imperial bloodline must remain pure. Centuries of inbreeding follow, slowly driving the emperors mad.
Founding of the Inquisition
In the south, Montego Valieda conquers the Castinellan Provinces in the name of the Arch Seraph Empyreus, declaring magic a heresy.

IV. The Gods' End

The Cataclysm
Emperor Leopold I succumbs to madness. The connection between him and the Gods corrupts the Divines.
• War in Heaven: The gods slay one another.
• Aurelia's Fall: The Goddess of Protection sacrifices herself to shield the world, crashing into the earth.

V. The Age of Darkness (Present)

The Darkfall
A massive earthquake strikes Ostoya. The sky turns permanently black. To save his people from the Bürach invasion, Captain Morgen Hund (now a vampire lord) turns the nobility into the undead.
The Weeping Pox
A magical plague released by the Filth Gazer decimates the city of Liesech. The physicians wear bird masks to hide the smell of death.
Rise of the Great Beast
A titanic aberration begins stalking the Bürach Empire. The Hearthkeepers claim the gods are merely "sleeping" to prevent mass panic.
Coldfire Crisis
In the north, blue fire begins consuming the Valikan lands, turning living flesh into ice. It is a sign that Gormadraug is waking up.
Lost to Time
  • The Liches' Sleep: The elven necromancers of Tol Kerdywel are not dead, merely sleeping in the mists.
  • The Dreamers: An ancient race that slept through the Aetheric War has recently awoken underground, with no memory of who they were.
  • The Downcast: Angels who survived the war were cast down to Etharis, becoming mortal wanderers stripped of their wings.

The Gods are dead. In their wake, their lieutenants—the Arch Seraphs and Arch Daemons—vie for the souls of mortals. Meanwhile, ancient powers that predate the gods stir in the dark.

Domain: Sacrifice & Endurance
Aphaeleon Temperance
